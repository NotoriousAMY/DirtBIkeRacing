Conceptual Data Model

Purpose:
This database captures dirt bike racing results across multiple race series in Western New York. It is designed to track racer performance over time, across events, classes, and series, wherever Amy Masters has raced and that class. Races include motocross and harescramble formats.

Entities and Descriptions: 
identity			Descriptions		
Racer               Person competiting in the race event on a dirt bike. 
Event				A race at a specific venue on a specific date.
Series				Collection of events organzied under one sanctioning body
Class				Registration cateogory based on gender/age/skill level
Results				Recorded outcome of the racer competing in an event.


Relationships: 
Racer — Result
A racer can have many results during there career. Each result belongs to one racer.
One Racer to Many Results 

Event — Result
An event produces many results across all classes and racers. Each result belongs to one event.
One Event to Many Results

Class — Result
A class can have many results within an event. Each result belongs to one class.
One Class to Many Results

Series — Event
A series is made up of many events throughout a season. Each event belongs to one series.
One Series to Many Events


Business Rules:
A racer may compete in multiple series in the same season
A racer may compete in one class at a single event
An event always belongs to exactly one series
A result must have a racer, an event, and a class to be valid
Place is recorded as a number or DNF (Did Not Finish)
A racer's bike brand may change between events


Out of Scope:
The following are not included in this model:

Lap by lap timing details
Racer age or license information
Financial or entry fee data
Team or sponsor information


Assumptions:

Racer identity is determined by name and hometown
Events are uniquely identified by series, venue, and date
Historical data spans 2019 through 2024
